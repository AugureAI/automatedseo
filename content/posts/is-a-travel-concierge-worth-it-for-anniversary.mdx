By the time you actually travel, you're mentally exhausted from the planning process itself.

## When to skip the concierge

Concierge service isn't universal necessity for anniversary travel. If you genuinely enjoy the research and planning process—comparing Airbnb properties in Savannah's Historic District or reading Wine Spectator reviews for Sonoma County restaurants—finding it relaxing rather than stressful, DIY planning might suit your relationship dynamic better. Some couples bond over the shared planning experience, treating destination research as part of the fun.

Similarly, if you're working within a $3,000 total budget for your anniversary trip where every $150 saved matters significantly, the time investment of personal research might be worthwhile. Budget anniversary travel to destinations like Asheville, North Carolina or Door County, Wisconsin requires different strategies than luxury celebration planning at The Post Ranch Inn, and the value of professional curation diminishes when options are limited by financial constraints rather than preference optimization.

Local or familiar destination celebrations also reduce the value of concierge expertise. If you're planning an anniversary weekend in Chicago and you already know the difference between The Chicago Cut Steakhouse and Gibsons Bar & Steakhouse, your local knowledge might exceed what a concierge can provide.
