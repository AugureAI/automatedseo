Paris doesn't require weeks to understand—it demands precision. Whether you have 48 hours or 4 days, the key isn't seeing everything but choosing the right everything. Skip the overwhelming listicles and choose your Paris based on what actually matters to you: romance, culture, or pure Parisian living.

---

## The romantic maximalist (2-3 days)

You want the Paris of movies and Instagram. This isn't about authenticity—it's about delivering on every romantic cliché with surgical precision.

**Stay:** Hotel des Grands Boulevards in the 2nd arrondissement. The lobby bar alone justifies the €380-450/night rate, and you're walking distance to both Palais-Royal and the covered passages. Alternative: Hotel Particulier Montmartre (€520/night) if you want boutique intimacy over central location.

**Day 1 priorities:**
- Sunrise at Trocadéro (6:47 AM in July, 8:23 AM in December—plan accordingly)
- Lunch at L'Ami Jean (book exactly 7 days out at 10 AM when they open phone lines, €45 for the sharing menu)
- Seine sunset cruise on Vedettes du Pont Neuf boats (€16, departures every 30 minutes), not the glass tourist barges

> "The difference between tourist Paris and romantic Paris is timing. Same places, different crowds."

**Day 2 non-negotiables:**
- Musée d'Orsay at 9:30 AM opening (skip the Louvre unless you have 4+ hours, €16 entry)
- Afternoon in Montmartre, but take the funicular up (€2.15) and walk down through the vineyard on Rue Saint-Vincent
- Dinner at Le Comptoir du 6ème—no reservations, arrive at 6:30 PM sharp for the €65 five-course menu

The romantic route requires strategic booking. Most top restaurants open reservations exactly 7-30 days out at 10 AM. The Eiffel Tower sparkles for 5 minutes every hour after sunset—plan your 8:30 PM dinner at Café de l'Homme accordingly (€180 per person, but you're paying for the view).

## The culture vulture (3-4 days)

You're here for art, architecture, and the intellectual weight of centuries. This Paris requires more patience and significantly more walking.

**Stay:** Hotel Malte Opera near République (€180-240/night). Less Instagram-worthy but you're equidistant from major museums and walking distance to the Marais. Superior rooms have original 1920s molding and decent-sized bathrooms.

**Museum strategy that actually works:**
- Louvre on Wednesday or Friday evening (until 9:45 PM, 70% fewer crowds, €17 entry)
- Musée Rodin Tuesday morning (€14 entry, the sculpture garden alone requires 90 minutes)
- Centre Pompidou Sunday morning before 11 AM (€16, contemporary art without the afternoon crowds)

> "Paris has 130 museums. The locals visit maybe six regularly. Choose quality over completeness."

**Beyond the obvious:**
- Musée Nissim de Camondo for perfect 18th-century interiors (€12, closes Mondays and Tuesdays)
- Sainte-Chapelle at 5 PM when the light hits the stained glass correctly (€11.50, combined ticket with Conciergerie saves €3)
- Père Lachaise cemetery—enter at Boulevard de Ménilmontant gate, not the tourist-heavy main entrance at Boulevard de Charonne

Culture Paris requires advance tickets for major exhibitions. Palais de Tokyo's temporary shows (€12) often outshine permanent collections. The Musée d'Orsay bookshop has better art books than most museums globally—budget extra time and €50-80 for purchases.

**Eating between museums:**
Skip museum cafés (overpriced at €18-25 for basic salads). Duck into any brasserie with a handwritten menu and zinc bar like Le Procope near the Louvre or Café de Flore near Musée d'Orsay. Order the daily special and a glass of Côtes du Rhône. Total damage: €22-28.

## The Parisian cosplayer (2-4 days)

You want to live like a local, even temporarily. This means markets, neighborhoods, and the rhythm of actual Parisian life.

**Stay:** Apartment rental in the 10th or 11th arrondissement (€120-180/night on Airbnb). Use the money saved on hotels for better meals and that Hermès scarf you've been eyeing at 24 Rue du Faubourg Saint-Honoré.

**Morning ritual:**
Start at your neighborhood boulangerie at 7:30 AM. Order "un café et un croissant" (€3.50 total) and eat it standing at the bar like every other Parisian. Du Pain et des Idées in the 3rd has the city's best pastries, but any place with a queue of locals at 8 AM works.

**Market days:**
- Marché Saint-Germain (Tuesday, Thursday, Saturday, 8 AM-1 PM)
- Marché des Enfants Rouges (Tuesday-Sunday, 8:30 AM-2 PM, oldest covered market in Paris, best falafel at L'As du Fallafel stand)
- Marché aux Puces Saint-Ouen flea markets (Saturday-Monday, serious dealers arrive Saturday at 6 AM, tourist prices start around 10 AM)

> "Real Parisian life happens in 20-minute increments: coffee, market, wine bar, repeat."

**Neighborhood deep-dives:**
Spend entire afternoons in single arrondissements. The 11th around Rue de Charonne for bars and vintage shops. The 5th near Place de la Contrescarpe for bookstores and student energy. The 18th along Rue des Martyrs for actual diversity and the city's best African food at Le Bumba or Nouveau Monde.

**Evening protocol:**
Apéro starts at 6 PM at wine bars like Le Mary Celeste or Septime Cave—one drink (€8-12), small plates (€6-14), conversation. Dinner not before 8:30 PM. Never eat before 7:30 PM unless you want to dine exclusively with tourists.

**Shopping like you live here:**
Monoprix for basics and decent wine under €8. BHV Marais for home goods and kitchen supplies. Merci concept store at 111 Boulevard Beaumarchais for gifts that don't scream "I bought this in Paris." The Marais on Sunday when everywhere else closes—but expect crowds around Place des Vosges.

## The logistics that matter

**Transportation wisdom:**
Skip the tourist metro passes unless staying 5+ days. Individual tickets cost €2.15 or use contactless payment (same price). The metro runs until 1:15 AM (2:15 AM Friday-Saturday). Uber works perfectly but expect €12-18 for cross-town rides versus €8-12 for taxis during peak hours.

**Money reality:**
Budget €120-180/day for quality meals (€25-35 lunch, €55-75 dinner, €15-20 for wine). Museum passes cost €30 for two days but save significant queue time at major sites. Decent wine starts at €6/bottle at Nicolas wine shops, €8-12 at Cave du Panthéon for natural wines.

**Weather planning:**
Paris weather changes every 20 minutes from October-April. Pack layers, always. Umbrella from any Monoprix (€12), not a raincoat. Comfortable walking shoes—you'll log 15,000+ steps daily. Parisians dress better than you—embrace it, don't fight it.

**Booking strategy:**
Reserve restaurants the moment you land. Popular bistros like Bistrot Paul Bert or Frenchie book 1-2 weeks ahead. Hotel bars at places like Hotel Costes don't take reservations but arrive before 7 PM or after 10 PM for tables.
