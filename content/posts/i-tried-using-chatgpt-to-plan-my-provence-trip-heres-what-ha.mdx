## The ChatGPT promise vs reality

I started with high hopes. ChatGPT painted a beautiful picture of my June Provence adventure: sunrise hot air balloon rides over Luberon Valley, truffle hunting with trained dogs in Carpentras markets, wine tasting at Domaine de la Janasse in Châteauneuf-du-Pape. The itinerary read like a Condé Nast feature.

Then I asked for specific flights from JFK to Marseille. ChatGPT confidently suggested Delta's "daily direct service" for around $650 round-trip. Spoiler alert: Delta doesn't fly direct to Marseille, and June flights were actually starting at $1,247 with connections through Paris or Amsterdam.

The hotel recommendations were equally problematic. That charming boutique property Les Bories & Spa in Gordes? Booked solid through August. The "affordable" farmhouse stay Mas de l'Oulivié in L'Isle-sur-la-Sorgue? The address led to a residential street with no hotel in sight.

> "ChatGPT's travel advice feels like getting restaurant recommendations from someone who's never actually eaten there—it sounds delicious until you try to make a reservation."

## Where general AI breaks down for travel

ChatGPT's training data has a fatal flaw for travel planning: it's not real-time. When I asked about current Air France availability, it hallucinated schedules from 2023. Hotel prices were pure fantasy—estimating €180/night for Hotel Crillon le Brave when actual June rates started at €385.

The bigger issue is that ChatGPT can't actually book anything. After generating my dream itinerary, I faced the tedious reality of checking every recommendation manually. Each "quick" hotel search turned into a 20-minute rabbit hole across Booking.com, hotel websites, and TripAdvisor reviews.

I spent three hours trying to coordinate flights that would connect properly with my ground transportation. ChatGPT kept suggesting impossible layovers (35 minutes in Charles de Gaulle for international connections) or flights that didn't operate on my travel dates—like recommending United's seasonal Nice route that doesn't start until July.

The final straw was discovering that my carefully planned June 15-22 dates coincided with peak wedding season in Provence—meaning villa rentals were 3x more expensive than ChatGPT's estimates and the best properties were booked 18 months in advance.

## The expertise gap

Travel planning requires industry knowledge that general AI simply doesn't possess. When I mentioned wanting to maximize my Delta SkyMiles, ChatGPT suggested booking Delta-coded flights. But it didn't know that Air France operates the actual JFK-CDG route as flight AF007, or that I could earn 1.5x miles by booking through Virgin Atlantic's partnership for the same flight.

ChatGPT also missed crucial context about Provence timing. It enthusiastically recommended visiting Sénanque Abbey's lavender fields without mentioning that peak bloom varies by elevation—lower fields near Valensole plateau peak in late June, while higher elevation Abbey fields don't peak until mid-July.

The AI couldn't advise on practical logistics either. Should I rent a car at Marseille Provence Airport or take the €8.30 train to Aix-en-Provence TGV and rent there? Which rental companies allow cross-border driving for my planned day trip to Sanremo, Italy? ChatGPT provided generic advice but couldn't access current Europcar or Sixt policies.
