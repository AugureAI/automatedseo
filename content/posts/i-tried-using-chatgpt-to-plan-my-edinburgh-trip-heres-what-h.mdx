ChatGPT is brilliant at brainstorming travel ideas—it gave me specific Edinburgh restaurant recommendations like The Witchery by the Castle and helped me craft a detailed three-day itinerary. But when it came time to actually book flights and hotels, I hit a wall. The AI couldn't check real prices, verify availability, or complete any bookings, leaving me to manually search dozens of sites for the options it suggested.

I spent my weekend planning what should have been a straightforward long weekend in Scotland, and learned exactly where general AI shines—and where it falls frustratingly short.

---

## The brainstorming was genuinely impressive

ChatGPT excelled at the creative aspects of trip planning. When I asked for authentic restaurants in Edinburgh that locals frequent, it delivered specific recommendations like Ondine on George IV Bridge for seafood and The Scran & Scallie in Stockbridge for modern Scottish cuisine. It even suggested booking Ondine for 7:30pm to avoid the pre-theater rush and mentioned that both restaurants get completely booked during Festival season in August.

The itinerary suggestions were thoughtful too. Rather than the typical "visit Edinburgh Castle and walk the Royal Mile" advice, it recommended starting my morning at Arthur's Seat at 7am for sunrise photos, then exploring the Grassmarket by 9am before the tourist buses arrived. It suggested specific photo spots along the Closes off the Royal Mile and recommended timing my visit to St. Giles' Cathedral around their 4pm evensong service.

> "ChatGPT is like having a well-read friend who's great at ideas but terrible at following through."

Where it really impressed me was understanding context. When I mentioned I'd be visiting in November, it automatically adjusted recommendations for the 8am sunrise and 4pm sunset. The Scotch Whisky Experience on Castlehill moved higher up the list, and it recommended The Bow Bar on West Bow and Deacon's House Café on Brodie's Close as cozy warming spots near major attractions.

---

## Then reality hit

The moment I tried to move from planning to booking, everything fell apart. ChatGPT confidently suggested flights from LAX to Edinburgh with "prices typically around $800-1200," but when I checked actual availability on November 15-18, every option ran $1,400-1,800. Even with a connection through London Heathrow, I couldn't find anything under $1,350.

Hotel recommendations came with zero price verification. The Witchery by the Castle sounded magical until I discovered their Gothic Suite runs £695 per night in November—slightly above my £150 nightly budget. The Scotsman Hotel on North Bridge was "moderately priced" according to ChatGPT but showed £280-340 for standard rooms on my dates.

The most frustrating part was the constant disclaimers. Every suggestion ended with "please verify prices and availability" or "I recommend checking directly with the provider." After the fifteenth reminder to double-check everything, I realized I was doing all the actual research anyway.

---

## The manual booking marathon

What followed was a tedious cycle of opening multiple browser tabs for every suggestion. I'd search Expedia, then Kayak, then Google Flights, trying to find the specific British Airways BA269 departure that ChatGPT recommended. Half the flights didn't exist on the dates I needed, and the others required piecing together information from different booking sites.

Hotel hunting was worse. I'd find The Inn on the Mile on the Royal Mile, discover it was fully booked November 16-17, then start the search process over again with Travelodge Edinburgh Central or Premier Inn Edinburgh City Centre. ChatGPT couldn't learn from these dead ends or adjust its recommendations based on what was actually available.

> "I spent more time verifying ChatGPT's suggestions than I would have spent researching the trip myself from scratch."

The breaking point came when I found a perfect combination—Virgin Atlantic flight VS24 at $1,420 and Brooks Hotel Edinburgh at £145 per night—only to have the flight sell out while I was comparing hotel cancellation policies on another site. ChatGPT had given me the inspiration, but I was flying solo on the execution.
